Robot Summer

Over the summer, I worked in a team of four to build a fully autonomous robot which navigated a course to pick up objects and deposit them in a safe area. We won third place in the robot competition. Over the course of this project, I designed, modelled and built a mechanical claw with four degrees of freedom; iterating the design using laser cut and 3D printed pieces.

Mechanical Troubleshooting:
- It was very challenging to properly constrain the vertical housing given the desired speed of the vertical stage, and I iterated through different kinds of press fits, a set screw, and finally had success with a hardboard casing with perpendicular ribs underneath for structural support.
- Another challenge I faced was the overconstrained horizontal platform, which I was able to mitigate by adding long 3D printed bearings along the guide rails which only contacted the rods 25mm below the platform.
- The lab-provided motors only barely provided the torque we needed to run the rotational and vertical stages, so we ended up sourcing our own

I also contributed to the software, which was written in c++ to coordinate driving, claw actuation, and object detection (with LiDAR and a magnetometer) using an ESP-32 microcontroller.

Concrete Canoe

From 2023 to 2025 I was a member of the Concrete Canoe hull design team and a structural analysis lead. In this role, I learned naval architectural theory to iterate a computer model of the surface of a canoe in Freeship, improving the maneuverability and decreasing turbulent flow (to improve straighline speed). I also performed structural analysis on the canoe design using Solidworks, RISA and excel. In addition to the standard load cases, I researched and developed a model for the stresses developed in the concrete when the canoe capsizes and must be towed to shore. I also worked with team members to cast, cure, sand and repair the canoe, receiving second overall at the 2024 ASCE Concrete Canoe Competition and eighth overall at the 2024 Canadian National Concrete Canoe Competition.

Fault Slip Potential Tool

In winter 2025, I did an internship at CNRL as an exploit student. While I was there, I built a fault slip analysis tool in python and excel which determines risk of slipping for different input fault parameters. The tool also plots each fault so that the pressure from the reservoir can be visualized geographically and on a Mohr's circle. Finally, a Monte Carlo analysis is run on the dataset to determine the likelihood of different output values due to uncertainty in the input parameters.
